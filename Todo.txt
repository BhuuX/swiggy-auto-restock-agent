What will make it stand out above other entries

A pantry-style household model
Don’t just read order history. Build a model like:
staples: milk, bread, eggs
weekly: fruits, curd, vegetables
monthly: detergent, tissues, cleaners
emergency items: diapers, medicine basics, pet food
This makes the agent feel like it understands the home.

Predicted depletion, not just “last ordered 7 days ago”
Your logic should combine:
order recency
usual quantity
frequency
item class
user household size
upcoming events
day-of-week patterns
Example:

milk every 3 days
bread every 5 days
detergent every 28 days
bananas only on weekdays
That looks much smarter than a simple cron rule.

Explainable decisions
Every recommendation should have a human reason:
“You usually buy 2 liters of milk every 3 days. Last purchase was 4 days ago.”
“Your monthly detergent cycle suggests you may run out this week.”
“You often pair bread with eggs and milk on Sunday restocks.”
That creates trust.

Substitution intelligence
This is a huge differentiator.
When preferred item is unavailable:
choose same brand smaller pack
choose same quantity different brand
ask user only when confidence is low
This makes the agent resilient and practical.

Budget-aware restocking
Add:
monthly grocery budget
soft cap
“urgent only” mode
“save money” mode
“brand-loyal” mode
Then the agent can say:

“To stay under your Rs 800 quick-restock budget, I removed non-urgent snacks.”
“I found a lower-cost alternative for detergent.”
That feels product-grade.

Schedule and mode controls
